Questions That Always Interest Me
Question 1: What is the most effective and scalable path to truly robust autonomous navigation?

Should we pursue the perfection of multi-modal sensor fusion for maximum redundancy, as seen in systems with LiDAR, radar, and cameras? Or should we champion the development of advanced, vision-centric AI that can overcome the inherent ambiguities of the physical world through superior reasoning, an approach famously adopted by Tesla? This question about the fundamental trade-off between hardware robustness and algorithmic intelligence is central to the future of autonomy of either vehicles or humanoid robots.

Question 2: Can robots reliably follow natural-language instruction? Are robots ready for language?

Google's RT-2 model and PaLM-E achieves over 80% success on unseen tabletop commands. And LM-Nav uses a frozen large language model to plan indoor routes. These results suggest language can already lower data needs and expand task sets, yet brittle perception under occlusion, limited long-horizon reasoning, and safety guarantees remain open problems. Investigating uncertainty-aware planners, larger real-world benchmarks, and manipulation task generalization is the direction I plan to pursue.
