Questions That Always Interest Me
Question 1: What is the most effective and scalable path to robust autonomous navigation?

Should we rely on rich multi-sensor fusion (like LiDAR, radar, and cameras) to reduce uncertainty through redundancy? Or should we focus on building a powerful vision-centric AI that learns an internal world model to reason about physics, predict motion, and handle ambiguity from raw images alone, just as Tesla FSD attempts to do? This trade-off between hardware reliability and learned understanding defines the future of how robots perceive, reason, and move through the world, the foundation of what we call "Spatial Intelligence".

Question 2: Can we build a semantic control platform for robots, specifically mobile manipulation?

As Vision-Language-Action models (like pi-0, pi-0.5) advance, robots are beginning to understand natural instructions such as “pick up the red mug”. However, turning this semantic understanding into reliable control under perception and reasoning uncertainty remains an open challenge. I am interested in whether we can develop a modular and uncertainty-aware control framework that unifies perception, reasoning, and action.
